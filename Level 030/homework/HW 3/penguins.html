Mr. Popper's Penguins

"Mr. Popper's Penguins" is a 2011 family comedy film based on the 1938 children's book of the same name by Richard and Florence Atwater. Directed by Mark Waters, the film stars Jim Carrey as Mr. Popper, a wealthy but disconnected businessman who finds his life turned upside down when he inherits six penguins from his estranged father. The penguins' arrival forces Mr. Popper to reassess his priorities, leading to comedic situations and heartfelt moments as he learns to connect with his family and embrace a more fulfilling life.

The film is known for its humorous portrayal of penguin antics and Carrey's energetic performance, blending slapstick comedy with family-friendly themes.
