Despicable Me

"Despicable Me" is a beloved animated film franchise produced by Illumination Entertainment and distributed by Universal Pictures. The first film, released on July 9, 2010, was directed by Pierre Coffin and Chris Renaud. It tells the story of Gru, a supervillain who adopts three orphaned girls—Margo, Edith, and Agnes—as part of his plan to steal the moon. However, Gru's life changes as he grows to love the girls and becomes a more caring person.

The film is known for its humor, heartwarming moments, and the introduction of the Minions, small yellow creatures who assist Gru and became cultural icons. The success of the first movie led to several sequels and spin-offs, making Despicable Me one of the most popular animated franchises of all time.
